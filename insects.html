Earwig

Earwigs make up the insect order Dermaptera. With about 2,000 species in 12 families, [1] they are one of the smaller insect orders. Earwigs have characteristic cerci, a pair of forcep-like pincers on their abdomen, [2] and membranous wings folded underneath short, rarely used forewings, hence the scientific order name, "skin wings". [3] Some groups are tiny parasites on mammals and lack the typical pincers. Earwigs are found on all continents except Antarctica.

Lacewings

Our Green Lacewings (Mallada signatus) are hardy, [1] generalist predators that feed on many small insects including aphids, caterpillars, moth eggs, scale insects, mealybugs, psyllids, and lace bugs. [2] They are among the most common and widely distributed native lacewings in Australia. [3] Adults are green, 15 mm long, and have large lacey wings
